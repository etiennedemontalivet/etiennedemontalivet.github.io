I work since June 2021 at Clinatec, a medical research laboratory in the CEA. I work specifically on seizure forecasting for a project that aims at developing and testing a neuroprosthesis to apply a cooling therapy to inhibit seizures.

Labellisation

I have used the most common state-of the art approach to label the data. From the database are only annotated the seizures and the subclinical seizures. Seizure forecasting consist in classifying a preictal state from an interictal state. Interictal state is often refered as a normal state opposed to the preictal period in which we hope to find biomarkers, precursors of the coming seizure. The preictal duration is choosen by researcher and I tried with 1 hour/15min.

It is important to note that this labellisation applies only on training. Once the binary classifer is trained, I tested on all the data of one group using Leave One Out approach, one group being made of data from postictal to the next seizure.

Metrics

Metrics are crucial in Machine Learning. A lot of scientific papers use the AUC in seizure forecasting to evaluate their models. It is relevant but has some limitations. First, it does not reflect the capacity to learn a threshold from a training/validation set to the test set. Secondly, it only applies on interictal and preictal labels, but remember that we have a lot more data. Finally, and maybe most importantly, we do seizure forecasting not to achieve the best AUC, but to create a system for people with epilepsy. This system could be a warning system, a closed-loop cooling therapy system, or whatever. In any case, this system will have timing constaints. For example, you probably don't want your alarm system to change the output every 5 seconds.

Those reasons lead to the use of 3 metrics on top of the AUC:

  • Time In Warning: this is the total time of preictal detection, including all data.
  • Sensitivity: this is the percentage of predicted seizures.
  • Improvement Over Change: this is the improvement over a Random Process Poisson classifier that has the same Time In Warning duration. See [1] for more details.
See [1] for more details on seizure forecasting metrics.

[1] Snyder DE, Echauz J, Grimes DB, Litt B. The statistics of a practical seizure warning system. J Neural Eng. 2008 Dec; 5(4):392-401. doi: 10.1088/1741-2560/5/4/004
